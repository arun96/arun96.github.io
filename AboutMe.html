About Me

A brief introduction

Hello! My name is Arun Das (he/him).

I'm a scientist with an interest in computer science and genomics. I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the Institute for Genome Sciences (IGS), inside the University of Maryland School of Medicine (UMSOM). Previously, I was a PhD student at Johns Hopkins University in Baltimore, Maryland, where I studied Computer Science and Genomics under the supervision of my advisor Dr. Michael Schatz. I defended my PhD in May 2025.

My research interests are algorithms for navigating and analyzing large genomic datasets, machine learning approaches to help handle large data, and cryptography and privacy. I am extremely passionate about education, accessibility and representation in genomics, and my work has been focused on understanding and bringing down the barriers to genomic analysis and research.

Before starting my PhD at Johns Hopkins, I studied Computer Science and Computational Biology at Brown University. I graduated with a Bachelors of Science, with Honors, in 2018. My thesis was on approaches in genomic privacy, and was supervised by my advisor, Dr. Sorin Istrail.

To find out how to get in touch with me, please use this page.
